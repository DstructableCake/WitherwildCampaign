# Cinematic Art Prompts for WitherWild Campaign Moments

Detailed image generation prompts for key campaign moments, formatted for LLM image generators with complete context for all elements.

## 1. Gnirth Accidentally Shatters the Prayer Statue

**Scene:** The ruined chapel of Monks Eliegh at dawn, a stone chapel with rows of wooden pews covered in thick dust. The chapel interior is dimly lit by a single candle burning steadily at a stone altar. Rows of petrified stone statues—men, women, and children—kneel frozen in mid-prayer throughout the nave, their faces worn with grief, hands clasped, eyes turned toward the altar. The air is dry and heavy, carrying the faint scent of stone dust and incense. Morning light filters through cracked stained glass windows, casting colored beams across the dusty floor.

**Characters:**
- **Gnirth (Thring):** A tall Fungril sorcerer with pale fungal skin that appears translucent, watery veins pulsing faintly beneath the surface. He wears a fungus cap on his head, cloaded shadowy figure. His eyes swirl like storm-tossed pools. He wears simple traveler's robes stained with bog mud and faint dust traces. His form is lean and ritualistic.
- **Mathias Vanta:** A gaunt, weary priest in his late middle age, with sunken eyes and patched, worn robes. He kneels at the altar among the stone figures, his voice a low murmur of endless prayer.
- **Sabriel Arden:** A broad-shouldered human knight in his late thirties, wearing scarred bronze armor etched with serpent insignia of the Knights of the Stone Serpent order.
- **Fianna Fernwhisperer:** A faun druid with tawny furred legs ending in cloven hooves, a bronze circlet resting between short antlers, and auburn hair in braids.

**Action:** Gnirth reaches out with curiosity toward one of the kneeling stone statues—a weathered figure of a middle-aged man frozen in prayer. The moment his pale fingertips brush the statue's weathered stone cheek, the head fractures with a brittle snap, shattering into powder and fragments that bounce across the cold flagstone floor. The sound echoes through the silent chapel like a gunshot. Gnirth's expression shifts from curiosity to horror as stone dust drifts through the morning light beams. Sabriel and Fianna stand in the background, their faces frozen in a mixture of shock and "did that just happen?" disbelief.

**Mood:** Bittersweet gallows humor in a sacred space, the weight of grief interrupted by accidental destruction.

**Style:** Realistic narrative painting with subtle exaggeration, highly detailed, atmospheric tension.

**Color Palette:** Cool grays of weathered stone statues, warm golden candlelight from the altar, pale teal undertones of Gnirth's fungal skin, muted browns of wooden pews, soft colored light from stained glass windows.

**Lighting:** Slanted morning sunlight filtering through cracked stained glass windows, creating colored beams that highlight drifting dust motes. The single candle at the altar provides warm golden accent lighting, casting long shadows across the stone figures.

## 2. Sabriel at the Stone-Frozen Dining Table

**Scene:** Inside Sabriel Arden's abandoned family home in Monks Eliegh—a small, well-kept frontier house that stands as an island of care in a sea of stone ruins. The interior is a simple dining room with a wooden table set for a family meal. Morning sunlight filters through warped wooden shutters, casting long shadows across the room. The table is fully set with simple crockery—clay plates, wooden utensils, a bread basket, and a pitcher—all covered in a fine layer of dust. Through the window, petrified neighbors can be seen standing in silent prayer in the streets outside, reinforcing the suffocating stillness of the abandoned town.

**Characters:**
- **Sabriel Arden:** A weathered human knight in his late thirties, broad-shouldered and battle-scarred. He wears scarred bronze armor etched with the serpent insignia of the Knights of the Stone Serpent, but his helmet is removed and placed beside him. His face shows the weight of survivors' guilt, gaunt with exhaustion. His gauntleted hands rest on the table beside untouched crockery.
- **Three Stone Statues (Family):** At the table sit three petrified stone figures—Sabriel's parents and sibling—frozen mid-meal. Their stone forms show the same grief-worn features as the other statues in town, forks lifted forever toward mouths that will never eat. The father figure has a strong jaw and broad shoulders, the mother has gentle features frozen in concern, and the sibling appears younger, perhaps a teenager.

**Action:** Sabriel sits at the head of the family dining table, his bronze armor catching the morning light. A simple prayer candle flickers between him and the stone figures of his family, casting long shadows across the neat but dust-coated room. His expression is one of profound grief and reverence—not weeping, but carrying the weight of impossible loss. He doesn't touch the food, doesn't move, simply sits in vigil with his stone family. The scene captures an intimate moment of survivors' guilt and remembrance.

**Mood:** Intimate grief, survivors' guilt, quiet reverence for the lost.

**Style:** Realistic interior painting with chiaroscuro focus, highly detailed, emotionally resonant.

**Color Palette:** Muted browns of weathered wooden furniture, cool marble grays of petrified stone flesh, warm bronze highlights on Sabriel's armor, soft autumn gold from morning sunlight, warm amber from the flickering candle.

**Lighting:** Single window beam creating dramatic side lighting, with the candle providing warm accent glow. Deep shadows pool in the corners, isolating Sabriel and his stone family in a pool of light. The contrast between warm candlelight and cool stone emphasizes the divide between living and petrified.

## 3. Fianna Comforts the Withered Bear

**Scene:** A misty thicket along a Nightveil trail—the strange perpetual twilight where stars burn brighter and the air feels thick. The forest undergrowth is damp and cool, with shafts of starlight cutting through the canopy. The ground is soft with moss and fallen leaves. Bioluminescent fungi flicker like false hopes in the shadows, their pale light creating an otherworldly atmosphere. The air carries the scent of damp earth, decay, and something acrid—the corruption.

**Characters:**
- **Fianna Fernwhisperer:** A faun druid with tawny furred legs ending in cloven hooves, a bronze circlet resting between short antlers, and auburn hair in braids. She wears a pale cloak speckled with dew and forest detritus. Her expression is one of profound empathy and sorrow.
- **The Corrupted Bear:** A massive brown bear lying on its side in the thicket, its body showing the devastating effects of Witherwild corruption. Black, oozing veins spread like ink through its fur, particularly visible on its extremities—paws, muzzle, and patches along its flanks. The corruption has a glossy, tar-like appearance. The bear's eyes reflect pain and confusion, but also a glimmer of understanding and gratitude.
- **Sabriel Arden:** Standing ready in the background, a human knight in bronze armor, his sword lowered but ready.
- **Snicket Surefoot:** A small blue-skinned goblin rogue in a black hooded jacket, purple hair visible, standing alert with daggers ready.

**Action:** Fianna kneels at the bear's massive head, her forehead pressed against its coarse brown fur. Tears stream down her cheeks, catching the faint bioluminescent light. Her hands gently stroke the bear's muzzle as she whispers words of comfort and promise—a promise of peace, of release from pain. The bear's eyes meet hers, showing gratitude despite the corruption consuming its body. Behind her, Sabriel and Snicket stand ready with weapons, but their expressions show they understand this is a moment of mercy, not combat. The bear's breathing is labored, and black ooze seeps from wounds where the corruption has broken through the skin.

**Mood:** Tragic mercy, profound empathy, the weight of necessary violence for compassion.

**Style:** Realistic wilderness tableau with emotional focus, highly detailed, atmospheric.

**Color Palette:** Damp forest greens of moss and undergrowth, deep purples and blues of Nightveil twilight, warm earth tones of Fianna's faun features and the bear's fur, glossy jet-black of the corruption ooze, soft bioluminescent blues and greens from nearby fungi.

**Lighting:** Diffused twilight glow from the perpetual Nightveil sky, with soft bioluminescent motes from nearby fungi creating ethereal highlights. The tears on Fianna's face catch the faint light, and the bear's eyes reflect both starlight and the corruption's dark sheen.

## 4. Sabriel, Snicket, and Gnirth Fight the Inferni Assassins

**Scene:** The Old Weir—a moss-choked, abandoned stone structure with a cave interior. The cave is dimly lit by flickering torches mounted on wet stone walls, their light reflecting off pools of stagnant water and moss-covered surfaces. The air is thick with the smell of damp iron, old decisions, and now smoke from smoke bombs. Stone supports groan overhead, and dust falls from the ceiling as the structure begins to collapse. The cave mouth shows daylight beyond, but the interior is a chaotic battlefield.

**Characters:**
- **Sabriel Arden:** A human knight in battered bronze armor etched with serpent insignia. His shield is raised, and his longsword gleams in the torchlight. He stands front and center, having activated his "Unstoppable" ability—his form radiates determination and protective fury.
- **Snicket Surefoot:** A small blue-skinned goblin rogue, approximately 3-4 feet tall, with purple hair and stone-scarred ear tips where piercings with chains run between them. She wears a black hooded jacket and dust-filtering bandana. She moves with shadowy grace, twin obsidian daggers flashing.
- **Gnirth (Thring):** A tall Fungril sorcerer with pale fungal skin showing watery veins, eyes swirling like storm-tossed pools. He channels elemental water magic, steam and boiling mist erupting from his hands.
- **Four Inferni Assassins:** Humanoid figures with sharp canine teeth, pointed ears, and horns (varying from two to four horns, some asymmetrically curved). They have long fingers with pointed nails, and some have long thin tails ending in points or arrowheads. Their skin shows various colors—rosy scarlet, deep purple, pitch black. They wear charcoal-colored leather armor and wield curved blades. Their "dread visage" is active, making their features more menacing—lengthened teeth, glowing eyes, twisted horns. Black veins pulse beneath their skin, showing Witherwild corruption.

**Action:** The cave explodes into violent chaos. Smoke bombs fill the air with thick gray clouds. Sabriel charges forward with shield raised, sparks flying where his sword meets an assassin's curved blade. His armor shows fresh dents and scratches. Snicket materializes from shadow behind the lead assassin, her obsidian dagger plunging into his ribs with precision. Her purple hair trails as she moves, and her blue skin contrasts sharply with the warm torchlight. Gnirth looms behind, his pale fungal skin glowing faintly as he channels boiling steam that melts another assassin's mask, the creature's face contorting in agony. Torchlight ricochets off wet stone, creating dramatic highlights and deep shadows. One assassin works at the cave mouth, setting charges that crack the supports, starting a countdown to collapse.

**Mood:** Cinematic melee combat, desperate teamwork, life-or-death stakes.

**Style:** Realistic action painting with dynamic motion, highly detailed, gritty texture.

**Color Palette:** Fiery oranges and reds from torch flames, slick moss greens of cave walls, metallic silver flashes from clashing blades, cool blues and purples of Snicket's skin and hair, pale teal undertones of Gnirth's fungal form, charcoal grays of assassin leathers, glossy black of corruption veins.

**Lighting:** Harsh torchlight creating dramatic chiaroscuro, with sparks from blade clashes adding strobing highlights. Smoke creates depth and obscures details in the background. The cave mouth provides backlighting, silhouetting figures in the foreground.

## 5. Fianna the Warhorse Crashes with Fern and Kreil

**Scene:** The same Old Weir cave interior, now in even greater chaos. Smoke fills the air, obscuring vision. Torchlight flickers wildly as the structure groans and begins to collapse. Stone debris falls from the ceiling, and the ground shifts underfoot. Embers from torches scatter like fireflies. The cave mouth is visible in the distance, but the path is blocked by combat and falling stone.

**Characters:**
- **Fianna (Transformed):** In her horse form, she is a massive white-flecked faun horse—her lower body has transformed into powerful equine legs and hooves, while her upper torso remains partially faun with her bronze circlet still perched between short antlers. Her auburn braids flow behind her as she runs. Her form is sleek and muscular, built for speed.
- **Fern Wingsteria:** A Fungril-faerie hedge witch with translucent bioluminescent wings that glow teal and lavender. She has permanent dark stains under her violet eyes. She clings desperately to Fianna's back, her wings flared in panic.
- **Kreil Durn:** A gaunt human spymaster in his 50s, with ash-gray stubble, ink-stained fingers, and a layered travel cloak. He carries a satchel stuffed with scrolls and papers. His form is sickly and thin, and he clutches Fianna's mane with one hand while holding his satchel with the other. A functional cane is strapped to his side.

**Action:** Fianna gallops at full speed through the chaotic cave, her hooves kicking up sparks and embers. Fern and Kreil cling to her back, their faces showing panic and determination. As Fianna attempts to navigate around falling debris and combat, she misjudges a turn and slams broadside into a crumbling stone wall. The impact is captured in a freeze-frame moment: chips of limestone and fragments of parchment from Kreil's satchel float in the air. Fern's bioluminescent wings flare wide as she's thrown forward, and Kreil's satchel spills scrolls that catch the torchlight. The wall cracks under the impact, and dust explodes outward. It's a moment of heroic intent gone comically, tragically wrong—the desperate escape attempt meeting the harsh reality of the collapsing cave.

**Mood:** Frantic heroism tinged with dark comedy, the slapstick misery of a plan gone sideways.

**Style:** Realistic yet kinetic snapshot, capturing motion in suspended time, highly detailed.

**Color Palette:** Pale creamy whites of Fianna's horse coat, bioluminescent teal and lavender from Fern's wings, muted rust browns of Kreil's travel cloak, warm oranges from torch flames, cool grays of limestone, creamy yellows of scattered parchment.

**Lighting:** Torchlight creates dramatic rim lighting on the figures, with embers providing strobing highlights. The impact creates a flash of light, freezing the moment like a flash photograph. Dust particles catch and refract the light, creating a hazy atmosphere.

## 6. Snicket's Shadowstep Escape from the Cave Collapse

**Scene:** The Old Weir cave is in the final moments of collapse. Massive stone supports groan and crack overhead. Fissures race across the ceiling, and boulders begin to cascade down. The cave mouth is visible ahead, showing daylight beyond—the promise of safety. Dust fills the air, and the sound of grinding stone is deafening. The ground shifts and cracks underfoot.

**Characters:**
- **Snicket Surefoot:** A small blue-skinned goblin rogue, approximately 3-4 feet tall, with purple hair and stone-scarred ear tips where piercings with chains run between them. She wears a black hooded jacket and dust-filtering bandana. She carries twin obsidian daggers. Her expression shows determination and fear.

**Action:** Snicket sprints toward the cave mouth, her small form moving with desperate agility. Her obsidian daggers flash in the dim light. Behind her, a massive ceiling slab breaks free and plummets toward where she just was. Just as the boulder would crush her, she twists mid-stride, her body dissolving into inky shadow ribbons—her form becoming semi-transparent, flowing like liquid darkness. The shadow ribbons re-form several yards ahead, and Snicket materializes whole, stumbling forward but alive. Dust clouds billow past where she was, and Sabriel—silhouetted by the collapsing tunnel behind—reaches toward her disappearing outline, his expression one of relief and awe. The moment captures the heart-pounding near-miss, the magical agility that saved her life.

**Mood:** Heart-pounding escape, magical prowess, the razor's edge between death and survival.

**Style:** Realistic action freeze-frame with motion blur effects, highly detailed, dynamic composition.

**Color Palette:** Smoky grays and charcoals of collapsing stone, ember reds from torch flames, cool cyan of Snicket's blue skin, deep violet of her hair, inky blacks of her shadow magic, warm gold from daylight at the cave mouth.

**Lighting:** Backlit by the cave entrance's daylight, creating a golden rim around Snicket's form. The shadow magic creates pockets of absolute darkness that absorb light. Golden dust motes swirl around her re-materializing form, caught in the light beams. The collapsing tunnel behind creates dramatic shadows.

## 7. Gnirth Interrogates the Inferni Prisoner

**Scene:** Back in the dust-choked Old Weir cave chamber, after the collapse has settled. The air is still thick with dust motes catching the light. The cave is dimly lit by flickering torches, their light reflecting off wet stone walls and pools of stagnant water. A blood-streaked stone pillar stands in the center, and the ground is littered with debris from the battle and collapse.

**Characters:**
- **Gnirth (Thring):** A tall Fungril sorcerer with pale fungal skin showing watery veins pulsing faintly beneath translucent flesh. His eyes swirl like storm-tossed pools, showing the "void" stirring within. He wears simple traveler's robes stained with bog mud. His expression is intense, driven by uncontrollable urges.
- **The Inferni Prisoner:** A humanoid figure hogtied and kneeling beside the blood-streaked pillar. He has sharp canine teeth, pointed ears, and horns (two asymmetrically curved). His skin is pallid with black veins pulsing beneath the surface, showing Witherwild corruption. He wears torn charcoal leather armor. His expression shows defiance mixed with pain. His long fingers are bound, and pointed nails are visible.
- **Fern Wingsteria:** Standing warily in the background, a Fungril-faerie with bioluminescent wings, watching with concern.
- **Snicket Surefoot:** A small blue goblin rogue in black, also watching from the shadows.

**Action:** Gnirth looms over the kneeling prisoner, his pale fungal form catching the torchlight. In one hand, he grips a glass jar of Witherwild tar—a viscous black substance that shimmers like a bruise, refracting the torchlight with an oily sheen. Wisps of steam curl from the stone floor around the prisoner's face as Gnirth channels scalding water magic, not directly hitting the prisoner but creating a threatening display. The steam rises in tendrils, and the prisoner's skin glistens with sweat and fear. Gnirth's eyes swirl with storm-tossed intensity, and his voice is low and rumbling like water churning over hidden depths. The jar of tar serves as both threat and tool—a reminder of what the corruption can do. Fern and Snicket watch from deeper in the cave shadows, their expressions showing wariness and moral conflict.

**Mood:** Menacing interrogation, morally gray territory, the darkness within confronting external evil.

**Style:** Realistic interrogation scene with heavy atmosphere, highly detailed, psychologically tense.

**Color Palette:** Deep blacks and browns of the tar jar, muted ochres and grays of stone cave walls, pale aqua undertones of Gnirth's fungal skin, ashy pallor of the prisoner's corrupted flesh, warm oranges from torch flames, glossy black of corruption veins.

**Lighting:** Low torchlight creating harsh shadows across the prisoner's face, with the jar of tar catching and refracting light in an unsettling way. Gnirth's form is backlit by torchlight, creating a menacing silhouette. Steam from the water magic catches the light, creating ethereal tendrils. Deep shadows pool in the background where Fern and Snicket stand.

## 8. Fianna Gallops to the Barracks with Sabriel and the Prisoner

The road is a dirt track, well-worn but rough. Late afternoon sunlight casts long shadows, and the sky shows gathering storm clouds in the distance. The Haven border barracks are visible on the horizon—a palisade of wooden walls with watchtowers, flags flying in the wind.

**Characters:**
- **Fianna (Transformed):** In her horse form, she is a massive white-flecked faun horse with powerful equine legs and hooves. Her upper torso remains partially faun, and her bronze circlet still perches between short antlers. Her auburn braids flow behind her as she runs. Her form is sleek, muscular, and built for endurance. Her breath comes in visible puffs in the cool air.
- **Sabriel Arden:** A human knight in bronze armor etched with serpent insignia, riding behind the prisoner. His helmet is on, and his longsword is sheathed but ready. His armor gleams in the sunlight, and his expression is determined and vigilant.
- **The Inferni Prisoner:** The same humanoid figure from the interrogation, now bound upright on Fianna's back, gagged with a cloth. His hands are tied behind him, and ropes secure him to the horse. His expression shows defiance and exhaustion. Black veins still pulse beneath his pallid skin. His horns are visible, and his pointed ears catch the wind.

**Action:** Fianna gallops at full speed down the fen road, her powerful legs churning up mud and water. Her hooves splash through puddles, sending sprays of water and mud arcing behind her. Sabriel rides behind the prisoner, one hand gripping the ropes that bind the captive, the other holding Fianna's mane for balance. The prisoner sways with the motion, his bound form jostling with each stride. In the distance, the barracks palisade grows closer, and figures can be seen moving along the walls—guards on watch. The sky above shows the promise of an approaching storm, with dark clouds gathering. The scene captures urgency, determination, and the controlled power of Fianna's transformation carrying them toward safety and justice.

**Mood:** Urgency and determination, the controlled power of transformation, racing against time and weather.

**Style:** Realistic travel panorama with dynamic motion, highly detailed, cinematic scope.

**Color Palette:** Sunlit golds and ambers of late afternoon reeds, creamy whites and browns of Fianna's horse coat, warm bronze of Sabriel's armor, moody grays of approaching storm clouds, deep greens of marsh vegetation, ashy pallor of the prisoner's skin.

**Lighting:** Warm side lighting from the low afternoon sun, creating long shadows stretching across the muddy track. The approaching storm clouds create dramatic contrast in the sky. Fianna's form catches the sunlight, and water droplets from splashing puddles refract the light into prismatic highlights. The barracks in the distance are backlit, creating a silhouette against the sky.

## 9. Fern, Snicket, and Gnirth Meet Fulg, Stacker of Stones

**Scene:** A mossy roadside clearing along a forest trail, with overcast midday light filtering through the canopy above. The ground is soft with damp earth and fallen leaves. A small cairn of stacked river stones sits to the side of the road—unremarkable at first glance, but arranged with intentional balance. Wildflowers grow around the cairn, and small puddles from recent rain dot the ground. The air is cool and carries the scent of damp earth and moss.

**Characters:**
- **Fern Wingsteria:** A Fungril-faerie hedge witch with translucent bioluminescent wings that glow teal and lavender. She has permanent dark stains under her violet eyes. She hovers slightly above the mud, having just placed the final stone in the cairn.
- **Snicket Surefoot:** A small blue-skinned goblin rogue, approximately 3-4 feet tall, with purple hair and stone-scarred ear tips. She wears a black hooded jacket. Her expression shows skepticism and disbelief, mouth slightly agape.
- **Gnirth (Thring):** A tall Fungril sorcerer with pale fungal skin showing watery veins. He folds his arms, his expression showing clear skepticism. His eyes swirl faintly.
- **Fulg, Stacker of Stones:** A squat, troll-like figure approximately 4 feet tall, made entirely of stacking stones—layers of river rocks, shale, and smooth stones that form a humanoid shape. He has mossy "hair" made of lichen and small plants growing between stones, and glowing pebble eyes that shine with amber light. His form is sturdy and grounded, with stones that seem to shift and settle as he moves. His voice, when he speaks, is like a gravel slide—deep and resonant.

**Action:** The cairn of stones begins to vibrate with a low, resonant hum. The stones don't collapse but instead tumble and roll, clicking into place with a forward motion. The transformation is magical but grounded—stones rearranging themselves into a seated, rocky figure. Fulg settles into a comfortable position at the roadside, his glowing pebble eyes regarding the party with patient calm. Fern hovers nearby, her bioluminescent wings casting soft light. Snicket's expression is one of complete disbelief—she's convinced it must be a trick, her goblin skepticism warring with the evidence before her. Gnirth stands with arms folded, his skepticism clear in his posture. Small wildflowers around Fulg's form bend toward him, and faint vibrations ripple through nearby puddles as his gravelly voice resonates. The moment captures the intersection of the mundane and the divine, the reluctant faith required to accept the impossible.

**Mood:** Awe and curiosity, reluctant faith, the intersection of the mundane and the divine.

**Style:** Realistic mythic encounter, highly detailed, grounded fantasy.

**Color Palette:** Damp greens and browns of forest floor and moss, bioluminescent teal and lavender from Fern's wings, cool cyan of Snicket's blue skin, deep violet of her hair, pale aqua undertones of Gnirth's fungal form, earthy grays and browns of Fulg's stone body, amber glow from Fulg's pebble eyes, soft pastels of wildflowers.

**Lighting:** Diffused midday light filtering through overcast sky and canopy, creating soft, even illumination. Fern's bioluminescent wings provide additional cool light. Fulg's glowing pebble eyes create warm amber highlights. The overall lighting is naturalistic, grounding the magical moment in reality.

## 10. Fern's Vision of the Returning Divinity

**Scene:** Inside Monks Eliegh's chapel during Nightveil—the perpetual starry twilight. The chapel interior is dimly lit by a single candle burning at the stone altar, its flame steady. Rows of petrified stone statues kneel in prayer throughout the nave, their forms casting long shadows. Dust lies thick on the wooden pews. The air is heavy with the scent of incense and stone dust. But reality is peeling away, creating a double-exposure effect where two worlds overlap.

**Characters:**
- **Fern Wingsteria (Physical Form):** A Fungril-faerie hedge witch kneeling within a chalk summoning circle drawn on the stone floor. Her bioluminescent wings are folded, and her eyes have rolled back to show only whites. Black tar drips from her mouth, eyes, and fingertips, pooling on the floor. Her body is rigid, caught in a trance. Her permanent dark stains under her eyes are visible. Her form is pale, almost translucent with strain.
- **Fern (Vision Form):** A spectral, semi-transparent version of Fern appears in the vision world, her form glowing faintly.
- **Mathias Vanta:** The gaunt priest stands at the edge of the scene, his expression one of helpless concern, his patched robes visible.
- **Sabriel Arden:** The knight stands ready but powerless, his bronze armor catching the candlelight.

**Vision World:** An impossible landscape overlays the chapel—a vast black ocean of tar-like pitch that stretches to infinity. The "water" is thick, viscous, and moves with malevolent purpose. Above, the sky is not a normal sky but a canvas of shifting auroras—strange stars that burn and move in living, restless currents of light. The colors are ultraviolet, toxic greens, and impossible purples. A small shattered island breaks the horizon, its edges sharp and crystalline. Perched on the island's highest ridge is a colossal toad—easily the size of a building. The toad's eyes are deep wells, black holes that seem to drink the starlight and aurora above. The creature's form pulses with ancient power.

**Action:** Fern's physical body kneels rigid in the chapel, black tar streaming from her orifices. The summoning circle around her glows with faint light, and chalk lines begin to crack. In the vision overlay, her spectral form crawls onto the shattered island's shore, lungs burning, reaching toward the colossal toad. The toad's abyssal eyes turn toward her, and the draw is immediate—a gravitational pull that threatens to consume her soul. Then the voice comes from everywhere: "WHO DARES SUMMON ME" — the words reverberate through reality itself, ripping cracks of light through the tar sea. The universe seems to press down, and the island begins to shatter into fragments of light. Fern's consciousness slams back into her body. In the physical world, spores and candle flames whirl in an unnatural gale, and Mathias and Sabriel blur at the edges, unable to help. Fern collapses unconscious, the echo of that cosmic voice lingering like a bruise on the air.

**Mood:** Cosmic dread, overwhelming divinity, the price of forbidden knowledge, the terror of awakening ancient power.

**Style:** Realistic surrealism with high drama, double-exposure effect showing two worlds, highly detailed, psychologically intense.

**Color Palette:** Inky blacks and deep browns of the tar ocean, toxic greens and ultraviolet purples of the aurora sky, pale whites and grays of Fern's strained physical form, warm amber of candlelight, cool teal and lavender hints from Fern's bioluminescence, impossible colors of the vision world that don't exist in nature.

**Lighting:** Mix of warm candlelight from the chapel and impossible celestial glow from the vision world, creating a double-exposure effect. The toad's abyssal eyes absorb light, creating voids of absolute darkness. Cracks of light rip through the tar sea, providing stark contrast. The overall effect is disorienting, showing two realities bleeding into each other.

## Notes for Image Generation

- All prompts assume the image generator has no context about the campaign, so every element (characters, creatures, locations, abilities, items) is fully described.
- Each prompt includes: Scene setting, Character descriptions, Action/moment, Mood, Style, Color Palette, and Lighting.
- The style is consistently "realistic" or "realistic fantasy" to maintain visual coherence across the campaign.
- Time of day, weather, and environmental details are specified to ensure consistency.
- Character appearances are based on their official descriptions from PC and NPC files.
- Creature and monster descriptions come from SRD files and session notes.
- Locations are described based on campaign location files and session descriptions.
