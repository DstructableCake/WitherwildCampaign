# Mr. Tilde

## Playing Character

Mr. Tilde is a Galapa fixer and black market broker who operates in Alula's Shadowreach district. Galapa fixer, broker, black market operator. Snicket's contact for package delivery. Pragmatic, risk-averse but opportunistic, plays both sides. Information source and black market access.

- Roleplay notes: Pragmatic, risk-averse but opportunistic, plays both sides. Black market operator who trades in goods, information, and safe passage. Snicket's contact for package delivery. May have information about Theodore, rebel network, or other threads.
- Signature line: "Everything has a price, and every price can be negotiated. The question is: what are you willing to pay?"

## History / Info

Mr. Tilde has operated as a black market broker in Alula for years, building a network of contacts and establishing himself as someone who can get things done—for a price. He operates from his den in Shadowreach (Tree 5B), where he trades in goods, information, and safe passage.

He is Snicket's contact for a package delivery, making him a key destination for her quest. He plays both sides of the conflict, trading with rebels, Haven sympathizers, and anyone else who can pay. His pragmatic approach means he will help whoever keeps trade flowing and the Wither off his doorstep.

## Appearance

Galapa fixer with the calculating eyes of someone who makes their living by knowing what people want and what they're willing to pay. As a Galapa, they resemble an anthropomorphic turtle with a large, domed shell into which they can retract their head and limbs for defense. They stand approximately 4-6 feet in height, with their overall shape dictated by their turtle heritage.

Their shell and skin come in earth tones—shades of green and brown—with colorful and unique patterns decorating their shell, especially intricate designs that may have been enhanced or marked to show their trade connections. Their face shows the experience of someone who has learned to read situations and negotiate deals, with a bearing that suggests they are always assessing value and opportunity.

They dress in mismatched clothing that suggests someone who trades in various goods—perhaps items acquired through their black market operations, adapted to accommodate their shell. Their fingers (or claws) may be ink-stained from keeping records, and they may have a perpetual half-smile that suggests they know something you don't.

Their movements are deliberate and measured—Galapa move fairly slowly no matter their age—but purposeful, reflecting their role as a broker who has learned to work efficiently despite their natural pace. They carry themselves with the confidence of someone who knows they can provide what others need—for a price, and with the added security of being able to retract into their shell when defensive measures are needed.
